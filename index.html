About

Hello World! I am a Nunatsiavut Inuk originally from Happy Valley-Goose Bay, Labrador, but currently, my journey has led me to the city of St. John's, NL. I'm passionate about bridging my cultural roots with the dynamic world of technology and providing Indigenous communities and organizations with the ability to present themselves in this technology age.

My journey in Tech began when I embarked on an enriching 28-week journey with the Indigenous Friends Association (IFA) through their IndigiTECH program. During this immersive experience, I delved into the realm of technology, completing courses in Web Development and earning certificates in UX/UI through the esteemed Jumping Elephants. Additionally, I honed my digital skills further by participating in the Digital Skills for Entrepreneurship Program with Canada Learning Code.

The latter half of the IndigiTECH program was a pivotal moment for me, as I completed a 12-week internship in Front-End Web Development at IFA. This hands-on experience allowed me to apply my knowledge in a real-world setting, solidifying my passion for coding accessible and responsive websites.

My journey is a testament to the fusion of traditional values and cutting-edge technology. I am committed to weaving a digital tapestry that reflects both my cultural heritage and my proficiency in the tech world. Design and coding are not just my skills but also my tools for creating an online space that is both visually compelling and user-friendly.

Join me on this exciting journey as I continue to explore the intersection of culture, technology, and creativity. Together, let's build a digital landscape that celebrates diversity and accessibility.
