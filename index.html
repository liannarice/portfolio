About

Hello World! I'm Lianna, a Nunatsiavut Inuk living most of my life in Happy Valley-Goose Bay, Labrador, but I recently relocated to St. John's, NL to attend Memorial University. My passion lies in merging cultural roots with technology, empowering Indigenous communities in this digital age.

My tech journey began with a transformative 28-week IndigiTECH program at the Indigenous Friends Association (IFA). There, I immersed myself in web development, obtained a UX/UI certificate from Jumping Elephants, and completed Canada Learning Code's Digital Skills for Entrepreneureship Certificate Program. A pivotal moment came during a 12-week Front-End Web Development internship at IFA, solidifying my passion to coding accessible and responsive websites.

I am dedicated to weaving a digital tapestry that honors tradition and embraces technology. Design and coding aren't just skills but tools to create visually compelling and user-friendly online spaces. Join me as I explore the intersection of culture, technology, and creativity. Together, let's build a digital landscape that celebrates diversity and accessibility.
